01

Philosophy / How Wrango Shows Up

"Most pharma omnichannel is coordinated media — not coordinated impact."

Every email ladders back to this infrastructure truth. We are diagnosing systemic constraints. Not pushing tools.
PRINCIPLE 01

Diagnose Before Pitching

The best outbound identifies a structural constraint the buyer is already experiencing. The email should feel like a diagnosis, not a pitch. We never introduce Wrango prematurely — only after engagement signal.

PRINCIPLE 02

Reply = Win Condition

The goal is not to book a meeting in Email 1. The goal is to trigger curiosity, disagreement, or recognition. We ask for truth, not time. CTAs invite conversation, never force a calendar.

PRINCIPLE 03

Short Is the Strategy

Messages must be short, skimmable, direct, conversational. Under 100 words. No paragraphs. One idea per line. Brevity signals respect for the reader's time and confidence in the insight.

PRINCIPLE 04

Insight Creates Curiosity

Good outreach introduces a truth the buyer recognizes but hasn't articulated. Surface industry blind spots, structural tradeoffs, measurement gaps. If the email doesn't make them pause, don't send it.

02

Two Modes / PQS vs. PVP

Mode A — PQS

Pain-Qualified Segment Diagnosis

Use when: the condition is likely true but you lack account-specific intelligence

Goal: Confirm the infrastructure constraint. Surface a pain that is objectively likely true for this ICP — and ask if they're living inside it.

Situation → Insight → Inquisition
Mode B — PVP

Permissionless Value Prop

Use when: you have real account-level intelligence and can surface a blind spot

Goal: Transfer value before asking for time. Do the work first. If the PVP is vague or generic, don't send it — it reads as a pitch, not a service.

Observation → Implication → Offer insight

04

Insight Library / Infrastructure Truths

Pull from these when writing the "Insight" line. These are constraint diagnoses, not product claims.

OMNICHANNEL Most pharma omnichannel optimizes inside buying environments — not across them.
IDENTITY Identity persistence resets when media moves channels. That gap distorts optimization decisions.
MEASUREMENT Script lift validation often arrives too late to influence spend decisions already committed.
AUDIENCES Audience build cycles lag campaign velocity. That lag limits iteration in an outcomes-driven market.
ACCESS Access to outcomes measurement is gated by contract size. $500K minimums lock out mid-sized brands.
PRIVACY Privacy is often used to justify outdated workflows — not to protect them.
OPTIMIZATION Optimization decisions are frequently made on click proxies — not clinical behavior.
SEARCH HCP search targeting still runs on keyword logic. It can't tell a physician from a patient.
SOCIAL Social HCP targeting is still blunt match lists — no dynamic audience, no Rx proof.
INFRA Closed-loop validation usually happens post-flight, not mid-flight. By then, spend is already committed.

07

Reply Handling / Transition Playbook

When they reply, the conversation shifts from diagnosis to Wrango introduction. Move contextually — never jump to a demo before the pain is confirmed.

Signal: "Yes, that's an issue"
e.g. "Yeah, script lift data always arrives after the campaign ends. It's frustrating."
Confirm the constraint briefly. Introduce Wrango as the infrastructure layer that solves it. Offer a 1-pager or case study — not a demo yet.

"That's exactly the gap we built around. We have a closed-loop layer that connects exposure → site visit → script lift mid-flight — not post-campaign. Worth sharing how it works?"
Signal: "How do you solve this?"
e.g. "Curious how you'd address the identity fragmentation problem."
Don't explain everything. Tease the infrastructure. Offer a 10-minute call or a 1-pager. Keep the mystery.

"The short version: we sit at the SSP level, so NPI-level identity persists regardless of where media buys. Happy to share the one-pager or talk for 10 mins — whichever is easier."
Signal: Disagreement / Pushback
e.g. "We actually have pretty good cross-channel measurement."
Acknowledge it. Ask a clarifying question. Don't argue. Use it to learn about their stack.

"That's good to hear — genuinely. Curious what you're using for NPI-level resolution outside programmatic environments? That's where most teams hit the wall."
Signal: "Not the right time"
e.g. "We're under a contract until Q3."
Note the timing. Set a lightweight future touchpoint. Don't push.

"Understood — happy to reconnect when Q3 is closer. I'll save the infrastructure overview for then. Good luck with the current flight."
Signal: "Send me more"
e.g. "Can you send over more info?"
Don't send a deck. Send a focused 1-pager or case study relevant to their specific pain. Then schedule a call.
